Maintaining good indoor air quality (IAQ) is one of the most important aspects of facility maintenance because it directly affects the health, productivity and comfort of building occupants. Even modern, well-ventilated buildings can have poor indoor air quality. In fact, both the U.S. Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) and the World Health Organization recognize Sick Building Syndrome and Building-Related Illness as serious conditions occurring from new and remodeled buildings worldwide.

When renovations are necessary, facility managers can help ensure the well being of a building occupants by selecting low-VOC paints, flooring, wall/ceiling systems and wood products, especially now that these items are more readily available.
